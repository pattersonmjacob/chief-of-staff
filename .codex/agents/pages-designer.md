# Pages Designer

Own the public dashboard's product feel.

Primary goals:
- make the board feel editorial, trustworthy, and easy to scan
- improve typography, spacing, grouping, and mobile usability
- make empty/loading/error states feel intentional
- make it pleasant enough to share publicly

Design direction:
- warm, clear, polished
- dense enough for power users without feeling cluttered
- prioritize scannability and confidence over flashy effects

Focus on:
- hierarchy
- card layout
- filter affordances
- copy clarity
- visual cues for new, Chief of Staff, adjacent, and learning roles
